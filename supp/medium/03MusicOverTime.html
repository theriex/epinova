My older music is an anchor to my younger self and what the music meant to me then. Memories of what the world was like then and what I was like then are very relevant personally, but not musically (with allowances for lyric acceptability, social references and technology innovation). The context of music at the time of its release is important in terms of its societal and personal historical impact. Music can exist as simply a snapshot of another time, but for it to remain alive, you need to listen to it again. You need to hear and connect who you were to who you are, where you are going and why.
